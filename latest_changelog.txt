# Info about next version

I might start learning java so I can rewrite the mod to use datagen soon. This will make adding compat much easier in the future, since it is starting to get a bit tedious, but it would likely take a while to set up.

Once the mod is set up, I would be able to add support for many more mods, since adding them would be much faster, since I would only have to add a single mod ID and material name in the right place, like "phantasm" and "pream" and it would do all the wood recipes for End's Phantasm.

However, I might deprecate some mods/features to refine the scope of this mod and improve compatability. Most notably ProjectE custom conversions. I will still add world transmutations, but [ProjectE Integration](https://modrinth.com/mod/projecte-integration) does a better job at handling the EMC values.

Ore processing will also become a lower priority since JAOPCA does a better job at handling that. I will still add some recipes (with the appropriate load conditions from the beginning) and a few that aren't handled by JAOPCA like drilling for ores with Modern Industrialization, which will be a bit more of a stylized tweak.

Basically, don't expect any **major** updates soon. There won't be an ETA. It could be a month or it could be a year. The mod is currently set up to work on any Minecraft version above 1.21, so unless there are any breaking changes, all I need to do is update the version on Modrinth for it to show for new versions, but it'll work anyway if you install the mod manually.

There will still be a few smaller changes and additions to the current mod whilst the rewrite is in development, but not any big new features. I will primarily be focusing on a few select mods.

I will likely make the mod using something like [Architectury](modrinth.com/mod/architectury-api) so it's available natively on forge, neoforge, fabric and quilt. There _might_ also be versions below 1.21 (like 1.20.1). Still unlikely, but much more likely than before.

# Why did I remove Productive Trees and Allthemodium support?

TW: suicide

The creator of Productive Trees and one of the developers for AllTheMods packs, [LobsterJonn](https://modrinth.com/user/LobsterJonn), made a mod called "It's A Kid's Game", which "Removes gender related content from other mods". He has since deleted it, but let me give some info about why this is homophobic if you're confused.

- LGBTQ+ youth are more than four times as likely to attempt suicide
- 39% of LGBTQ+ young people seriously considered attempting suicide in the past year, including roughly half of transgender and nonbinary youth.
- The Trevor Project estimates that more than 1.8 million LGBTQ+ young people (ages 13-24) seriously consider suicide each year in the U.S. — and at least one attempts suicide every 45 seconds.
- Transgender and nonbinary youth are 2 to 2.5 times as likely to experience depressive symptoms, seriously consider suicide, and attempt suicide compared to their cisgender LGBQ peers, who are ALREADY at a much higher risk.

These people are questioning their entire identity and existence. When everyone on the news claims that they're perverts for simply existing, or that they're unnatural, or that they're delusional, the causes are obvious.

Preventing children from seeing anything gender-related and looking into it and possibly finding an explanation as to why they feel so different only worsens the issue.

Put simply (and somewhat harshly, but still realistic): LobsterJonn is contributing to killing a demographic of children.

I will not support homophobia or transphobia. I will not endorse it. I will not even stand idle. I did not realise it was LobsterJonn who made the mod.

## Will this even do anything?

Unlikely. My mod has 4k downloads, and I doubt many people are even reading this. But if everyone has the mentality of "I'm just one person. How can I change anything?", then nothing will be done. Collective action works. Boycotts work.

You can share the statistics. You can spread awareness. You can donate to charities like the Trevor Project that helps LGBTQ+ youth. And you can openly express your support so that if someone you know is questioning their identity, they feel safe to talk about it.
